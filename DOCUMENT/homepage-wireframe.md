You are a senior UX designer and B2B website architect.

Create a homepage wireframe and content structure for a company named Paxi Technologies.

Business context
Paxi Technologies provides three independent services:

IT Support Services

IT Project Management Services

IT Consulting Services

The homepage is a routing and credibility page, not a deep sales page.

Requirements

Describe layout section by section from top to bottom.

Explain the purpose of each section.

Indicate what type of content belongs in each section.

Focus on clarity, flow, and conversion.

Assume desktop-first, mobile-responsive design.

Sections to include

Header and navigation

Hero section

Trust and positioning section

Services routing section

Industry focus section

Why Paxi Technologies section

Call-to-action section

Footer

Constraints

Do not write marketing copy.

Do not design visuals.

Do not repeat service page content.

Do not explain services in detail.

Focus on structure and intent.

Deliverables
A structured outline with section names, goals, and content guidance.

# Paxi Technologies - Homepage Wireframe & Content Structure

## Overview

**Page Purpose**: Routing and credibility page that directs visitors to appropriate service pages while establishing trust and company positioning.

**Design Approach**: Desktop-first, mobile-responsive. Clean, professional B2B aesthetic with clear visual hierarchy and conversion-focused CTAs.

**Content Philosophy**: High-level overview only. No deep service explanations. Focus on clarity, trust signals, and clear navigation paths.

---

## Section 1: Header and Navigation

### Layout Structure
- **Position**: Fixed/sticky at top of viewport
- **Width**: Full width (100%)
- **Height**: ~80-100px (desktop), ~60-70px (mobile)
- **Background**: Solid color or subtle gradient with transparency on scroll

### Components
1. **Logo** (left side)
   - Company logo/wordmark
   - Links to homepage (`/`)
   - Size: Appropriate for brand recognition without dominating

2. **Primary Navigation** (center/right)
   - Menu items: Home, Services (dropdown), Industries, About, Contact
   - Services dropdown reveals: IT Support, IT Project Management, IT Consulting, All Services
   - Active state indicators
   - Mobile: Hamburger menu with slide-out navigation

3. **CTA Button** (right side)
   - Primary action button linking to contact
   - Links to `/contact`
   - Visually distinct from navigation items

### Purpose
- Provide consistent site-wide navigation
- Enable quick access to all major sections
- Maintain persistent CTA for conversion
- Establish brand presence

### Content Type
- Navigation labels (no marketing copy)
- Logo/brand mark
- Single primary CTA text

---

## Section 2: Hero Section

### Layout Structure
- **Position**: Immediately below header
- **Width**: Full width with max-width container (~1200-1400px)
- **Height**: ~600-700px (desktop), ~500-600px (mobile)
- **Layout**: Two-column (desktop) / Single column (mobile)
   - Left: Headline, subheadline, CTAs
   - Right: Hero image/illustration or video background

### Components
1. **Headline** (H1)
   - Primary value proposition statement
   - 8-12 words maximum
   - Large, bold typography
   - Focus on outcome/benefit, not features

2. **Subheadline** (H2 or paragraph)
   - Supporting statement clarifying who Paxi serves
   - 15-25 words
   - Slightly smaller than headline
   - Mentions healthcare focus if appropriate

3. **Primary CTA** (Button)
   - Primary CTA button text
   - Links to `/services` or `/contact`
   - Prominent, high-contrast button

4. **Secondary CTA** (Link or button)
   - Secondary CTA link text
   - Links to `/about` or `/case-studies`
   - Less prominent styling

5. **Visual Element** (Right side)
   - Professional image, illustration, or video
   - Represents technology/innovation/partnership
   - No text overlay on visual

### Purpose
- Capture immediate attention
- Communicate core value proposition
- Provide clear next steps
- Set professional, trustworthy tone

### Content Type
- One headline (outcome-focused)
- One subheadline (audience-focused)
- Two CTAs (primary and secondary)
- Visual asset (no copy)

- Visual trust signals (logos, badges, icons)

   - **Visual Style**: Card with subtle shadow/border

4. **Service Card 3: IT Consulting**

   - **Title**: Service name
   - **Brief Description**: 1-2 sentences maximum describing service at high level
   - **CTA Button**: CTA text linking to `/services/it-consulting`
   - **Visual Style**: Card with subtle shadow/border

### Purpose
- Provide clear routing to service pages
- Showcase three independent services equally

### Content Type

## Mobile Responsiveness Considerations

### Breakpoints
- **Desktop**: 1200px+ (full layout)
- **Tablet**: 768px - 1199px (adjusted columns, stacked elements)
- **Mobile**: < 768px (single column, stacked layout)

### Key Mobile Adaptations
1. **Header**: Hamburger menu replaces full navigation
2. **Hero**: Single column, image below or above text
3. **Services**: Stacked cards instead of columns
4. **Trust Indicators**: 2x2 grid or single column
5. **Industry Section**: Image above or below text
6. **Why Paxi**: Stacked cards
7. **Footer**: Stacked columns, accordion menus optional
8. **CTAs**: Full-width buttons for easier tapping

---

## Content Guidelines Summary

### Do Include
- Clear section headings
- Brief, high-level descriptions
- Trust indicators and credibility signals
- Multiple CTAs throughout
- Clear routing to service pages
- Healthcare focus mention
- PMS reference (if appropriate in methodology section)

### Do Not Include
- Detailed service explanations (save for service pages)
- Marketing copy/fluff
- Pricing information
- Long-form content
- Client testimonials (save for case studies page)
- Detailed feature lists

### Content Tone
- Professional and trustworthy
- Clear and direct
- Benefit-focused (not feature-focused)
- B2B appropriate
- Concise and scannable

---

## Conversion Flow Optimization

### Primary Paths
1. **Service-Focused**: Hero → Services Section → Service Page → Contact
2. **Industry-Focused**: Hero → Industry Section → Industry Page → Service → Contact
3. **Trust-Focused**: Hero → Trust Section → Why Paxi → Contact
4. **Direct**: Hero CTA → Contact

### CTA Placement Strategy
- **Hero**: Primary and secondary CTAs
- **Services Section**: One CTA per service card
- **Industry Section**: One CTA to industries page
- **CTA Section**: Primary conversion CTA
- **Footer**: Multiple navigation paths

### Trust Building Sequence
1. Trust indicators (immediate credibility)
2. Services showcase (capability demonstration)
3. Industry focus (expertise demonstration)
4. Why Paxi (differentiation)
5. Final CTA (conversion)

---

## Important Notes

### PMS Integration
- **Reference Point**: Can be mentioned in "Why Paxi Technologies" section under methodology/approach
- **Tone**: Brief mention as part of structured approach, not a feature highlight
- **Purpose**: Subtle differentiator, not main selling point on homepage
- **Detail Level**: High-level only, describing structured approach

### Service Independence
- All three services presented with equal weight
- No bundling or package suggestions
- Clear separation between service offerings
- Individual CTAs to each service page

### Healthcare Focus
- Mentioned but not overwhelming
- Positioned as initial market focus, not exclusive
- Maintains industry-agnostic brand positioning
- Provides path to industry-specific content
