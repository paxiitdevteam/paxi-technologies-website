# Paxi Technologies - Proposal-to-Contract Handoff Process

**Internal Use Only**

## Purpose

This process ensures clean transition from proposal acceptance to service delivery.

**Goals:**

- Prevent scope drift
- Preserve service boundaries
- Ensure delivery clarity
- Eliminate assumptions between sales and delivery

This process applies to all services:

- IT Support Services
- IT Project Management Services
- IT Consulting Services

---

## When This Process Starts

- Proposal accepted by client
- Written confirmation received
- No service work begins before completion

---

## Ownership

- **Sales owner** initiates handoff
- **Delivery owner** accepts handoff
- **Operations** oversees compliance

---

## Step 1. Proposal Acceptance Confirmation

### Checklist

- [ ] Client acceptance received in writing
- [ ] Proposal version confirmed
- [ ] Service type confirmed
- [ ] Scope confirmed
- [ ] No verbal promises outside proposal
- [ ] No service bundling implied

**If any item is unclear, stop and resolve before proceeding.**

---

## Step 2. Contract Alignment Check

### Checklist

- [ ] Contract matches proposal scope
- [ ] Service boundaries explicitly stated
- [ ] No execution included where excluded
- [ ] No consulting included where excluded
- [ ] No support included where excluded
- [ ] Term and start date aligned

**If contract deviates from proposal:**

- Stop
- Resolve discrepancy
- Reconfirm with client if required

---

## Step 3. Internal Handoff Preparation

Sales owner prepares handoff summary.

### Required Content

- Service type
- Client objectives
- Confirmed scope
- Explicit exclusions
- Key risks or sensitivities
- Client expectations flagged
- Decision authority identified
- Start date confirmed

### Checklist

- [ ] Discovery notes attached
- [ ] Final proposal attached
- [ ] Signed agreement attached
- [ ] Onboarding checklist assigned

---

## Step 4. Sales-to-Delivery Handoff Meeting

**Meeting required. No exceptions.**

### Agenda

- Client overview
- Why the service was selected
- What is in scope
- What is explicitly out of scope
- Boundary risks identified
- Client expectations discussed
- Delivery questions addressed

### Checklist

- [ ] Delivery owner understands scope
- [ ] Delivery owner confirms exclusions
- [ ] Boundary risks acknowledged
- [ ] Open questions documented
- [ ] Ownership accepted

**If delivery owner is unclear:**

- Stop
- Clarify before proceeding

---

## Step 5. Service Boundary Confirmation

Delivery owner reviews scope independently.

### Checklist

- [ ] Scope aligns with service definition
- [ ] No mixed service expectations
- [ ] No implied execution in consulting
- [ ] No implied support in projects
- [ ] No implied projects in support

**If boundary issues found:**

- Escalate to operations
- Do not begin delivery

---

## Step 6. Client Notification

Client communication sent after internal acceptance.

### Checklist

- [ ] Delivery owner introduced
- [ ] Service start date confirmed
- [ ] Service scope restated
- [ ] Boundaries restated
- [ ] Next step explained

**No new promises added.**

---

## Step 7. Onboarding Execution

Use correct onboarding checklist for service type.

- Do not mix checklists
- Do not shortcut steps

### Checklist

- [ ] Onboarding checklist assigned
- [ ] Onboarding owner confirmed
- [ ] Delivery start gated on completion

---

## Step 8. Handoff Completion Confirmation

### Checklist

- [ ] Delivery owner confirms readiness
- [ ] All documents filed
- [ ] Client notified
- [ ] Service start approved

**Only after this step may delivery begin.**

---

## Escalation Rules

### Escalate Immediately If

- Scope ambiguity exists
- Client expectations exceed proposal
- Service boundaries are challenged
- Verbal commitments conflict with documents
- Client requests bundled services

### Escalation Path

1. Operations manager
2. Leadership

**Document all escalations.**

---

## Stop Conditions

### Do Not Proceed If

- Proposal and contract differ
- Service type is unclear
- Scope is mixed
- Client assumes follow-on work
- Delivery owner does not accept scope

**Stopping early protects delivery.**

---

## Process Enforcement

- This process is mandatory
- No exceptions without leadership approval
- Skipping steps is a delivery risk

---

## Outcome

When followed correctly, this process ensures:

- Clean service starts
- Protected boundaries
- Predictable delivery
- Reduced rework
- Aligned client expectations
